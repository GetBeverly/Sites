Laws governing the pipeline

Office segmentation — one pipeline, multiple markets

Each Market Center powers one or more Business Centers (for example, MC Waco powers BC Temple). Command provides one pipeline interface per Market Center, so segmentation happens through mandatory office tags applied at the Recruit Lead stage — not through duplicate stages. Tags name the office directly: MC - Waco, BC - Temple, and so on. The Office tag drives SmartViews; the Assignee field identifies the recruiter. One clean pipeline structure across all offices, with side-by-side reporting via SmartViews.

Pipeline guardrails

Drawn from the Command Market Center Laws of the Pipeline and Full Audit Procedure.
Law 2Every interaction logged in detail. Notes, appointments, calls, and texts logged in Command by every leadership team member.
Law 4Every recruit has a next task. No recruit sits without a scheduled action.
Law 5Active Phase updated weekly. A new interaction or next step logged every week.
Law 6Leads Never Contacted = 0. The widget stays empty at all times.
Law 7Date confirmed = Appointment Set. Move the recruit the moment a date and time are booked.
Law 8Cultivate touched every 30 days. A logged interaction within the month at minimum.
Law 9No past Projected Join dates. If the date slips, reset it — never leave it stale.
Law 10No stagnant recruits. Anyone not moving gets advanced, re-tasked, or archived.
AuditAppointment Held ≤ 7 days. A held appointment with no decision must move within a week.
TagOffice tag at creation. Every recruit tagged with the Market Center or Business Center they belong to, as the first checklist item.
