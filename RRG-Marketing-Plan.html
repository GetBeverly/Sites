The Rincon Marketing System

"It's a Great Market for Smart People."

Three coordinated initiatives. One measurable outcome: Sienna-area listings and past-client referrals.

How It All Fits Together

  • Three initiatives, one objective: drive Sienna-area listings + past-client referrals through a measurable, repeatable system.
  • Initiative 1 — Marketing collateral that generates leads. 8-piece content library, 4–5 posts/week, cross-posted everywhere, all tied to Sienna activity.
  • Initiative 2 — Expired listings within 6 miles. Full Jimmy Mackin 7-letter sequence + door-drop, no cold calls, no emails. Two tracks: fresh expireds (empathy) and old expireds (Blue Ocean).
  • Initiative 3 — Monthly past-client email with rotating referral asks. Twelve emails/year, six-section format.
  • Live progress visible anytime — the This Week tab shows what's happening right now. The Scorecard tab shows how we're winning week-over-week.
Operating philosophy

"Every dollar and every hour should be accountable to a specific result we can measure week over week — not 'marketing presence' for its own sake."

Initiative 2

Expired Listings Within 6 Miles — The Engine

Two-track Mackin 7-letter sequence, no cold calls, hand-delivered first impression

The single highest-ROI source for Sienna-area listings is expired and terminated listings. Two pools, worked differently:

PoolApproach
Fresh expireds / terminated
(last 30 days)		 Empathy-first. Embarrassment, not anger. Acknowledge frustration before pitching.
Aged expireds
(1–5 years aged, never resold)		 Blue Ocean. Zero competition. Lead with "It's been X days since you listed... [Y] homes like yours have sold since." Handle the risk objection upfront.
The real addressable universe
Real MLS data shows ~475 expired + terminated + aged-never-resold properties in Sienna + Riverstone alone. Adding Glendale Lakes, Stonebrook, Steep Bank, and Fresno (the rest of the 6-mile radius) brings the addressable pool to 600+. Median list price $575K, average $706K — exactly the Mackin framework price-point match.

8 touches per prospect over 50 days. Zero phone calls. Zero cold emails. The first two touches (Letter 1 + Day-3 Packet) are hand-delivered for maximum perceived effort. Letters 2–7 are mailed. Every letter carries a QR code routing to a destination matched to the letter's theme. Letters 4, 5, and 7 carry Make-Offer-For-Immediate-Response offers — "waive pre-listing photography fee" / "free marketability audit by [date]" / "include staging consultation."

Why 7 letters, not 1 or 2
50% of sales happen after the 5th attempt. 92% of agents quit before they get there. Most expired campaigns send one letter and stop. This one sends seven, with a clear next-step on every single touch.

Initiative 3

Monthly Past-Client Email Requesting Referrals

12 emails/year, six-section format, rotating monthly ask

Built from scratch — each email is a designed deliverable. The KW Command Neighborhood Nurture runs as a separate parallel touch (auto-sends Command's templated monthly market data to past clients in 6 neighborhoods) but is not the spine of this email. Two different channels doing two different jobs.

The 6-Section Email Structure

  1. Personal note — 2–3 sentences. Real. In voice.
  2. Sienna + Houston market update — 3 bullets + chart. Sourced from monthly HAR data.
  3. Recent activity recap — Just Listed / Sold / Under Contract since last email.
  4. Featured content — alternates monthly between Sienna spotlight and Waterwood spotlight.
  5. The rotating monthly referral ask — see below.
  6. Footer — Referral capture form link + social links.
