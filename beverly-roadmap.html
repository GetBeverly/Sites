What Could Go Wrong (And How To Plan For It)

Five realistic failure modes. Mitigations baked in before they happen.

1. Al doesn't drive the door-drops consistently

The plan depends on him doing this. If he flakes on it, the highest-converting touch disappears (door-drop converts 3–5× higher than email per Glover U).

Mitigation: Make Tuesday "delivery day." Hand him a stack of 8–10 personalized packets every Tuesday with streets pre-mapped. Ask Friday if he got them all delivered. Track it in the scorecard.

2. HAR expired data doesn't flow weekly

If Al/TC misses the Monday HAR pull, your whole week of expired sequence work stalls.

Mitigation: Set a Sunday-night recurring calendar invite for Al/TC. Make this his habit, not yours to remember.

3. YellowLettersComplete or printer has a delay

Letters arrive 2–3 days late, throwing off the Mackin sequence timing.

Mitigation: Build a 5-business-day buffer into every send. Schedule Letter 1 for Day 1 mail-date not receive-date. Test cycle time with first batch.

4. You burn out doing too much

4 hours sustained 5 days a week is real labor. Especially in the first 90 days.

Mitigation: One full "no work" day per week (probably Sunday). Don't open Metricool, don't check the QR form. Recharge.

5. Al wants to add things mid-pilot

Once early results show up, expect Al to want more — new listings posts, more emails, helping with other agents at KW Advantage.

Mitigation: Anything beyond the scoped plan = new pilot fee or new retainer line. Hold the line nicely. "Happy to scope that as an add-on" is the answer.
